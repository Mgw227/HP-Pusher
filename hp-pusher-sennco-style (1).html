Improving Shelf Execution and Sales Productivity

HP Pusher™

Introducing the revolutionary HP (High Performance) Pusher — a game-changer for retail product merchandising. Unlike any other pusher on the market, the HP Pusher delivers consistent, controlled forward-facing for every product on your shelf, from the lightest single-serve can to the heaviest multi-pack case.

This heavy-duty pusher is remarkably adaptable, capable of smoothly and consistently facing both lightweight items and products weighing up to 40 lbs* — all at the same controlled speed. Available in Single Motor and Dual Motor configurations to match your category's exact demands.

Features & Benefits

Designed for High-Velocity
Beverage Aisles

The HP Pusher addresses the root cause of shelf execution breakdown in high-velocity categories. As customers pull product from deep refrigerated shelves, inventory migrates to the back — leaving empty facings that are invisible to store teams and distributors. The HP Pusher eliminates that problem entirely.

  • Constant-force heavy-duty spring mechanism for consistent shelf presence
  • Adjustable channel width to fit any can, bottle, or pack size
  • Compatible with standard 22" deep refrigerated cooler shelving
  • Integrated plastic dividers prevent cardboard pack edges from catching
  • Dual motor variant handles products up to 40 lbs at uniform push speed
  • No shelf modifications required — installs on existing gondola and cooler fixtures
  • Simplifies distributor stocking — channels provide clear product slots for one-can-at-a-time restocking
  • Reduces time searching for empty facings — field-reported 30+ minute labor savings per shift
  • Improves facing consistency and product availability throughout the day
  • Minimal training required — repeatable installation playbook for multi-store rollout

Single Motor

HP Pusher SM

The Single Motor HP Pusher is purpose-built for single-serve cans and standard-weight SKUs in high-velocity refrigerated sections. Its precision spring system maintains constant forward pressure across the full shelf depth, eliminating the gaps and scattered product that require manual recovery throughout the day.

Ideal for ready-to-drink beverages, energy drinks, sparkling water, and standard beverage cans in both door-cooler and gondola shelf formats.

  • Single-serve cans and standard-weight beverage SKUs
  • High-velocity RTD, seltzer, and energy drink sections
  • Configurable channel width for mixed SKU sets
  • Compatible with existing cooler shelving — no modifications
  • 7–8 SKUs per pusher channel at 22" depth
  • Supports 938–1,072 single cans per section (per Jewel-Osco pilot data)
Dual Motor

HP Pusher DM

The Dual Motor HP Pusher is engineered for case packs, 4-packs, and heavy multi-unit SKUs in deep refrigerated sections. Two independent spring motors work in tandem to deliver consistent forward pressure on products up to 40 lbs — with zero speed differential between heavy and light items.

Ideal for beer and seltzer case packs, multi-pack RTD beverages, and any cooler section carrying heavier product configurations.

  • Products up to 40 lbs pushed at uniform, controlled speed
  • Dual-motor system eliminates stalling on heavy SKUs
  • Case packs, 4-packs, 6-packs, and variety packs
  • Adjustable channel width fits standard and wide pack formats
  • 3–4 packs per pusher channel at 22" depth
  • Supports 225–300 packs per section (per Jewel-Osco pilot data)
How It Works

Four steps to a shelf that works for itself.

01
Install on Existing Shelving
The HP Pusher mounts directly to standard cooler and gondola shelving with no tools required and no shelf modifications needed.
02
Configure Your Channels
Adjust channel widths to match your specific SKUs — cans, bottles, or packs. Anti-snag dividers prevent cardboard from catching during stocking.
03
Load and Stock Normally
Distributors and store teams stock product into the channels as normal. The spring mechanism automatically positions every item at the front.
04
Product Always Faces Forward
As customers remove items, the spring pushes remaining product forward — maintaining full, consistent facings automatically throughout the day.
